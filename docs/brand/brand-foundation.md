## Name Meaning & Brand Rationale

The Arabic word **عَمَد** (Amad) means *pillars*, *columns*, or *structural supports* — the foundational elements that hold a building upright. In classical Arabic it also carries the meaning of intentionality and directedness: to move with purpose toward something.

The name was chosen for three reasons:

1. **Structural resonance.** Construction is built on pillars — physical, organisational, and financial. Amad names what holds everything together.
2. **Linguistic depth.** The word reads naturally in both Gulf Arabic and Modern Standard Arabic. It requires no explanation for the target market.
3. **Brand geometry.** The form of عَمَد generates a natural visual vocabulary of vertical structures and horizontal foundations — pillars and baselines — that informs every element of the identity system.

## Product Positioning

Amad is a **professional construction operations intelligence platform** for the Saudi and Gulf construction sector. It gives project owners, executives, and site teams a single command centre for real-time oversight of projects, procurement, safety, quality, and people.

Amad is not a generic project management tool. It is not a consumer app. It is not a dashboard template. It is enterprise-grade operational intelligence built specifically for the built environment.

**Category:** Enterprise construction intelligence software
**Market:** Saudi Arabia, GCC
**Maturity:** B2B SaaS, operations-critical

## Visual Principles

1. **Structure over decoration.** Every visual element has a purpose. Decorative elements that do not communicate are removed.
2. **Information hierarchy.** Numbers are the hero. Typography scale serves data readability, not style.
3. **Controlled restraint.** Gold is an accent, not a theme colour. Navy communicates authority, not heaviness.
4. **Grid discipline.** Spacing is consistent. Alignment is exact. Nothing floats.
5. **Dark mode parity.** Dark mode is not an afterthought. It is a full professional experience — deep navy, not black; gold, not neon.
6. **Bilingual integrity.** Arabic content is not a translation appendage. RTL layout is native. Arabic typography receives the same care as English.

## Voice and Tone Principles

**Voice:** Authoritative, direct, professional. Speaks to engineers and executives, not consumers.

**Tone in UI:** Short, precise, functional. No filler words. No exclamation marks. No casual language.

**Tone in documentation:** Clear and complete. Explains the what and why without unnecessary elaboration.

**Do:**
- "17 projects delayed." (factual, direct)
- "Access denied." (clear)
- "Select a project to view data." (instructional, not apologetic)

**Do not:**
- "Oops! Something went wrong." (casual)
- "Loading your amazing data…" (informal)
- "You don't have permission to see this yet." (vague)

## English Naming Rules

- Product name is always **Amad** (capitalised initial, lowercase remainder)
- Full name is **Amad Construction Intelligence** (three words, no hyphen)
- Never: AMAD (all caps in prose), amad (all lowercase in prose), Amad CI in user-facing copy
- In UI, use **Amad** as the short-form logo wordmark
- The wordmark SVG file uses tracked uppercase **AMAD** — this is a typographic treatment in the mark only

## Arabic Naming Rules

- Arabic name is always **عَمَد** (with the correct diacritical — fatha over ع)
- Never use the name without the diacritical in formal brand contexts
- In Arabic UI context, the full descriptor is **عَمَد للذكاء الإنشائي** (Amad Construction Intelligence)
- Arabic wordmark is set in Noto Sans Arabic 700 weight

## What Amad Is Not

To maintain brand integrity, explicitly avoid these associations in all communications and visual design:

- Generic AI platform (no neural network imagery, no "AI" in the logo)
- Construction helmet brand (no hard hats)
- Property / real estate brand (no house or roof silhouettes)
- Consumer productivity tool (no casual UI, no emoji, no rounded cartoon icons)
- Luxury design brand (no excessive gold, no fashion aesthetics)
- Futuristic tech brand (no neon, no circuit boards, no holographic UI)
